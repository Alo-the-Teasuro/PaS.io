03 — Metals

The tonnage bottleneck

Volume metals move on the buildout itself — wiring, busbars, heat sinks. These have liquid, chartable prices. The in-chip metals (gallium, hafnium, tungsten) are chokepoints, not trades: tiny tonnage, extreme supply concentration.

Grams, not tons

The metal inside a die is measured in grams — chips alone don't move copper markets. The tonnage lives in the racks, wiring, and cooling wrapped around them.

Packaging is the AI part

What separates an AI accelerator from a normal chip is everything below the die: the CoWoS interposer, HBM stacks, and power delivery. Packaging, not transistors, became the bottleneck.

Chokepoints, not trades

Gallium, hafnium, tungsten, helium: trivial tonnage, extreme supply concentration. They show up as risk in margins and lead times — not as a clean ticker.
